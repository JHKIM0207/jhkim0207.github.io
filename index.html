About

I am a Senior Researcher at the Heliospheric Research Center, Korea Astronomy and Space Science Institute (KASI). My research focuses on space environment, heliophysics, ionospheric physics, and space weather prediction. I received my Ph.D. from Chungnam National University with a dissertation on the development of a physics-based regional ionospheric model over the Korean Peninsula and neighboring regions. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

My work integrates physics-based modeling, data assimilation, satellite and ground-based observations, and AI-driven forecasting methods to improve understanding and prediction of the coupled magnetosphere-ionosphere-middle atmosphere system. I have worked on regional ionospheric models, space weather integrated modeling, and machine-learning approaches for forecasting ionospheric disturbances. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Currently, I am leading and contributing to the development of multi-scale ionospheric and space weather models, with particular interest in geomagnetic storm responses, auroral boundary estimation, ionospheric forecasting, and solar-terrestrial coupling. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

  • KIDARiM: development of a data-assimilative regional ionospheric prediction model, currently used operationally. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Observations and Data Experience

My research uses a broad range of observations from both space-borne and ground-based instruments, including TIMED/GUVI, DMSP/SSUSI, COSMIC, SWARM, GOES, ACE, MAVEN, ionosondes, digisondes, GNSS/UNAVCO TEC, Fabry-Perot interferometers, magnetometers, and SuperDARN data. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

AI and Deep Learning
